#### FEATURE
#### Fire on Lesvos again

The residents of Moria 2\.0 experienced another fire today, only months after the blaze that destroyed the first camp\. The residents [put out the fire](https://www.facebook.com/MoriaWhiteHelmets/posts/267932024849864) themselves, as the fire department arrived 10 minutes after the blaze had started\. Their work was difficult due to the lack of extinguishers and the cramped tents, making it easy for the fire to spread\. Another video of the fire \(and the firefighting efforts\) can be found [here](https://www.facebook.com/watch/?v=1429332300786481) , via Refocus Media Labs\.

The families living inside the tent that first caught fire [narrowly escaped with their lives](https://twitter.com/DunyaCollective/status/1362796073648082949?fbclid=IwAR2qLGDN9nM_6TEX-1QM2wD60wLXtsqzQmaxaZe27F6JyCcDRwEfiADi4Zc) and at least one person suffered burns\. Their tent was [completely destroyed](https://www.facebook.com/MoriaWhiteHelmets/posts/267958758180524) and everything they owned is gone\. Although the material damage caused by this fire was not as bad as the one that destroyed the original camp, it still took a psychological toll\. The blaze [brought up traumatic memories](https://twitter.com/f_grillmeier/status/1362808996986556426) for many residents\. Several people had serious reactions to the trauma, including shock, [fainting spells, and seizures](https://twitter.com/teammareliberum/status/1362789708670111746?fbclid=IwAR1x-ZWGhdS3mROTZSFPyxke_KaRBa-1f-hIWqEM4uPmORmG_i70y5FsVgU) \.

According to the local fire brigade, the fire was probably caused by a short circuit\. Although this time luckily there was not too much damage, an accident like this could easily happen again due to a the faulty electrical wiring in the camp‚Äîand next time people could get seriously hurt\. These are not safe conditions for people to live in\.

#### SYRIA
#### Syrian government extorting people who fled

In a widely circulated video, a senior Syrian government official said that the [government would seize assets](https://www.thenationalnews.com/opinion/comment/syria-s-cash-strapped-government-is-extorting-refugees-1.1167847?fbclid=IwAR2r7AUnWFrtfoUzK73JyeSfWfiUSDYcaMRB0iiiz8l5zE17ENei10GuNPM) from people who had not paid their military exemption fines and were older than 42\. #### SEA
#### Distress cases, rescues at sea

AlarmPhone received information about several distress cases today\. #### UK
#### Delay in asylum cases affects people‚Äôs employment rates

The lengthy time it takes the UK government to process applications for asylum causes people to have [serious trouble finding employment](https://refugee-rights.eu/2021/02/18/the-impact-of-asylum-decision-limbo-on-refugees-reduced-employment-rates/) \. About two thirds of people seeking asylum wait more than a year for their decision, and they are not allowed to work while they are waiting\. This means that even after they receive legal status in the UK, they have difficulty finding work because of the gaps in their work history\.

One such person is Stella\. Stella has been waiting for an interview with the Home Office, let alone a final decision, for over two years\. You can watch an interview with her [here](https://twitter.com/4refugeewomen/status/1362423063170740226?fbclid=IwAR0ng5AlqVzFYbzt1TrpcrIq0WsCbxgRK2--Gxz3j3KN6lqbYDerUOfJzhk) \.

The UK‚Äòs restrictions on people‚Äôs ability to work are the strictest in Europe\. For a long time, people on the move and their allies have been asking for these restrictions to be relaxed so that people can support themselves and integrate better into British society\.

At least eleven hotels for people on the move, most of them in London, are severely restricting the freedom of movement of their residents\. An anonymous person is [seeking justice from the Home Office](https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2021/feb/19/asylum-seeker-brings-case-against-covid-curfew-at-london-hotel?fbclid=IwAR3EJLuupbDX6_zSfvB4hon1i4oJD35EAwEVr1p2xaMPBns3IcrgNaacyWg) for those unjust restrictions, including a ‚Äúcurfew‚Äù that lasts twenty\-three hours a day and security guards at the door\. If people on the move take longer than an hour to do their essential shopping, then their asylum chances get affected\.

Local London councils and charities have already raised questions about the way people are treated in these hotels, citing tiny rooms and terrible food as cause for concern\. Meanwhile in Kent, doctors are continuing to speak out [against the use of the Napier Barracks\.](https://www.kentlive.news/news/kent-news/housing-asylum-seekers-folkestones-napier-5016682?fbclid=IwAR3TTONYvlQuQPphPX7lp6SvAtCQaWQR1E5u-McpWb0HUB00awRKnk1njus)

Last month, photographer Andy Aitchison was arrested for taking photos of a demonstration in front of the Napier Barracks\. Although the charges against him were dismissed, the police still issued him a [Fixed Penalty Notice](https://www.pressgazette.co.uk/photographer-andy-aitchison-covid-19-protest-fine/) for about two hundred pounds\. The police later walked back the fine and said it was given by mistake, but it seems suspiciously like an attempt to intimidate a journalist and curtail freedom of the press\.
