**EU proposes suspending the right to asylum due to ‚Äòinstrumentalisation‚Äô\.**

The European Council is discussing a mechanism whereby individual countries can suspend the right to asylum in order to mitigate the risks of ‚Äòinstrumentalisation‚Äô\.

This term, which the EU Commission is seeking to define, refers to a situation like that in Belarus, when people seeking safety were encouraged to enter the country in order to ‚Äúattack‚Äù the European Union\.

[ProAsyl delves deeper into potential problems](https://www.proasyl.de/news/gesetzesvorstoss-der-eu-sendet-verheerendes-signal-in-die-welt/?fbclid=IwAR2WLb2DMrIX5E1JTcoJFyMFGhNGfk5O8X00ZS8JAi46HTg1w8Dy3UPjqbg) , whilst civil groups have issued statements against the proposals\. A long list of NGOs, including the Border Violence Monitoring Network \(BVMN\) to which AYS belongs, appear at the bottom of [this letter](https://ecre.org/joint-statement-ngos-call-on-member-states-agreeing-on-the-instrumentalisation-regulation-will-be-the-final-blow-to-a-common-european-asylum-system-ceas-in-europe/?fbclid=IwAR3NBkKHjmiTbtc9kI99vmSRf2teTDTxkvBJ5lfiu9FiFPYlo1L1Vp86qa0) which clearly outlines all the problems\. An in depth analysis [can be found here](https://ecre.org/ecre-commentary-an-analysis-of-the-fiction-of-non-entry-as-appears-in-the-screening-regulation/?fbclid=IwAR1Mg114I0SAs3Ma5XkHha-Y9VxTT6xMdlSaS5HmUupLoCHxInyEhGZ6kZs) which describes how a ‚ÄòScreening Regulation‚Äô would see third country nationals treated as though they hadn‚Äôt fully entered the country\. The full [text from the European Commission can be found here\.](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX%3A52021PC0890&from=EN&fbclid=IwAR0wUTgq6OdsTsEbYMrGHVizxagltTRzqAQ7Em_Is6sWPaqvsKLMtHHvPk0)
#### TURKEY
#### Detention centres in Turkey under scrutiny

The International Refugee Rights Association has published a report on the conditions for people in Turkey\. The country hosts around 4 million people, the most of any country world\-wide\. The detention centres in the country vary in standards considerably, and IRRA and Global Detention Project call for those standards to be raised, ensuring that everyone has access to legal advice and representation and that the detention of unaccompanied minors, mothers with children and pregnant women is stopped ‚Äî this is against international law\. [Read the full statement here\.](https://www.globaldetentionproject.org/oral-submission-gdp-partner-irra-provides-input-on-issues-related-to-immigration-detention-in-turkiye-at-the-un-committee-on-migrant-workers-35th-session?fbclid=IwAR0v0VoT01K3Xi9sXtto1cM7Tvoj-fCmnqddOqO62XMgKjryAqnoKARDLHY)

#### More women and children are in need in Serbia\.

The border town of Subotica has seen an increase in people on the move over the last few months\. The rise in numbers has also led to an increase in women and children, whose needs are struggling to be met\. [Collective Aid reported](https://www.instagram.com/p/CiscLDctNPZ/?fbclid=IwAR2QhjJRr-qD2VQrbMBkI6LC5XTctr8iPcLYs4ycT6ySp1S1hfVTFm_5bck) that a child as young as four years old was beaten by Hungarian border authorities\. They also call for donations to better attend to injuries and other needs\.
#### LITHUANIA
#### Pushbacks from Lithuania causes mental and physical harm

Medical organisation MSF have issued a report describing conditions at the border between Lithuania and Belarus\. Their teams met people who were pushed between both countries for days on end\.


> ‚ÄúPeople who already faced several traumas such as sexual violence and torture in their country of origin are now faced with the new trauma of violent pushbacks,‚Äù [Heidi Berg](https://www.msf.org/repeated-pushbacks-cause-mental-and-physical-suffering-people-crossing-lithuanian-border?fbclid=IwAR1C_xGHIu8BETacTqIfTgeHl3D2FwA-FXZH6h5YN4T8urVGhpuIsKAM9ik) , MSF psychologist in Lithuania\. 





One group were in the border area for 30 days, crossing between the countries 10 times, unable to settle and provided with poor and insufficient food\.

The Lithuanian government has no intention of halting the practice of pushbacks, but rather is seeking to formalise it into law \(see EU section below on this\) \. [Approximately 4,200 people entered Lithuania](https://www.baltictimes.com/lithuania_s_pushback_policy_traumatizes_migrants_mentally__physically___msf/?fbclid=IwAR3WP9dhdSxGgZjLKKa6imfXd-CX8S6mcMNHI6knuXPt7nSytsU7fTNHoA0) from Belarus last year\.
#### SWITZERLAND
#### Restraints used on deportation flights from Switzerland

The National Commission for the Prevention of Torture \(NCPT\) [has reported](https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/restraints-still-used-during-forced-migrant-repatriation--says-watchdog/47879120?fbclid=IwAR25emvevSNigskJTnY_ToLZgJe7umFFKgu-DvERpicLQFDBM5_TwsYq7l0) on the continued use of physical restraints during ‚Äúforced repatriations‚Äù\. 33 deportation flights took off from Switzerland from April to December 2021, restraints were used in 62% of cases\.

These restraints were full or partial using handcuffs or belts, with the most shocking of incidents being that of a mother who had to breastfeed her baby in the presence of her other children whilst handcuffed\. Described as [‚Äúdegrading and inhumane‚Äù](https://www.schengenvisainfo.com/news/switzerland-ncpt-reports-on-33-forced-returns-by-air-that-took-place-in-2021/?fbclid=IwAR0mSLdOYFiosuzmhIPpyLURrhMkgYDuyak3JmO0TstvXQpuuBu3ODfMIuA) , the NCPT seek to remind authorities that restraints should only be used when the individual is likely to harm themselves or others\.
#### FRANCE
#### Court case continues: associations against the city of Calais\.

Two years ago, the provision of food and water in certain areas of the city was outlawed\. Since then, a group of organisations working in the area have made legal challenges to regain permission to provide basic necessities to up to 1,000 people in need in the area\. The court case was to resume on 20th September, the same day as a [mass eviction](https://www.instagram.com/p/Ciu3N0hrJwX/) took place at dawn in Loon\-Plage\.

The August report from the Human Rights Observers in northern France [has been published\.](https://www.instagram.com/p/CikKNuxj8Am/) The report, which is divided into the two key geographic areas, documents multiple evictions, destruction of property, as well as lack of access to water and hygiene measures\. [Available in multiple languages here\.](https://humanrightsobservers.org/fr/monthly-observations/?fbclid=IwAR1Tcqz5JaP3rxZZvN_uMH_1dAPjP8lPISVYdzbfKphGbv0Qxy-jiTZe2g0)

This comes as Interior Minister Gerard Darmanin denounces the living conditions for people on the move in northern France\. The question about the terrible conditions, as well as the harassment of organisations attempting to help came from Left Member of Parliament [Benjamin Lucas](https://twitter.com/Benjam1Lucas/status/1572266042637926400?s=20&t=ZhmWMU-Yd7ovvUmZOH-xZA) \. Darmanin denied the truth of living conditions, which are clearly documented, and his response is in turn denounced by Utopia 56\.

#### EU
