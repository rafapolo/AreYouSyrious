### FEATURE ‚Äî In historic ruling, Italian supreme court says opposing deportation to Libya is not a crime


![The Irish Naval Service rescuing people during Operation Triton in the summer of 2015 off the coast of Libya \(Photo: [Irish Defence Forces, CC\-BY\-20](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:LE_Eithne_Operation_Triton_(18833934641).jpg) \)](assets/e5836d0308b2/1*nntLi2f-LM6Xi2zvTRWO7Q.jpeg)

A highly\-anticipated court verdict was [announced](https://www.avvenire.it/attualita/pagine/la-cassazione-assolve-due-profughi-ribelli-e-condanna-i-respingimenti-in-libia) this Thursday by Italy‚Äôs highest court, which ruled in favor of two people on the move, saying that their actions taken to oppose their own deportation to Libya from an Italian ship back in 2018 were justifiable\.

The December 16 ruling from the Supreme Court of Cassation overturns a previous sentence issued by Palermo‚Äôs Court of Appeal, which had convicted the accused, known as ‚ÄúT\.‚Äù and ‚ÄúI\.‚Äù, for crimes of aiding and abetting illegal immigration, violence, and forceful resistance to a public official\. On June 3 of last year, that court had sentenced T\. and I\. to three years and six months in prison, with a fine of 52,000 euros\.

The defendants‚Äô lawyers, Fabio Lanfranca and Serena Romano, said in a [statement](https://mediterranearescue.org/en/news-en/vos-thalassa-case-historic-sentence-by-italian-high-court/?fbclid=IwAR1KuthyqeerPtTdsF48_2s3YnE3oN2UoSZDY8lsRexTvBpKG9i27_mMD9Y) that they were ‚Äúvery satisfied with this important statement‚Äù from the high court, which ‚Äúreaffirm\[s\] that the enforcement of human rights is based on the rules of international law which ensures the protection of human life and is not a state authority matter\.‚Äù

‚Äú\[R\]escue operations at sea which result in the return of survivors to Libya constitute a breach of the principle of non\-refoulement and violate the rights of the people rescued who should be taken to a place of safety where their lives are no longer in danger and respect of their fundamental rights is guaranteed,‚Äù the lawyers continued\.

The case stems from an incident on July 8, 2018 in the Central Mediterranean\. The Italian tugboat _Vos Thalassa_ \(which at the time was working at a Libyan oil platform\) informed the Maritime Rescue Coordination Center \(MRCC\) Rome \(the central sea rescue coordination center for Italy\) that it had rescued more than 60 people from a small wooden boat in the Central Mediterranean\. The MRCC forwarded this information to the Libyan authorities\. When they did not respond, the MRCC directed the _Vos Thalassa_ to head to Lampedusa\. But the commander of the _Vos Thalassa_ then told MRCC that the so\-called Libyan Coast Guard had ordered him to drive his ship towards Libya and transfer the people to a Libyan patrol boat\. The _Vos Thalassa_ started heading south\.

At this point, the rescued people began to understand they were being driven back to Libya, and demanded that the crew reverse course and head back north\. The commander of the _Vos Thalassa_ reported the tension to the MRCC and asked for a military unit to be sent\. Finally, the MRCC sent a naval unit of the Italian coastguard to the tugboat, which brought the rescued people to Italy\.

T\. and I\., considered the leaders of the ‚Äúrebellion,‚Äù were later charged with ‚Äúcrimes of violence or threat and resistance to public officials‚Äù and ‚Äúaggravated aiding and abetting irregular immigration‚Äù under Italian law\.

Italian politicians maligned the two young people at the time of their arrest\. Matteo Salvini, then Interior Minister, accused them of ‚Äúhijacking‚Äù the tugboat, and Danilo Toninelli, then Minister of Transport, called them ‚Äútroublemakers\.‚Äù

However, the crew of an Italian Navy helicopter sent to the _Vos Thalassa_ to quell the ‚Äúrebellion‚Äù have since said that ‚Äú\[t\]he situation was under control, the alarm was not justified, no danger neither for the crew nor for the migrants,‚Äù [media](https://www.avvenire.it/attualita/pagine/la-cassazione-assolve-due-profughi-ribelli-e-condanna-i-respingimenti-in-libia) reported\.

‚Äú\[T\]he two young people, who had escaped from the Libyan hell, had acted in order to save themselves and the other shipwrecked people from risk of suffering new, very serious injuries to the rights to life, physical and sexual integrity, to protect their prerogative to be taken to a place of safety and to obtain international protection,‚Äù the lawyers, Lanfranca and Romano, told media this week\.
