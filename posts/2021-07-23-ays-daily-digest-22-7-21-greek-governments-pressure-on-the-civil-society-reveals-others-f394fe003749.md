#### FEATURE ‚Äî **Greek government targets organizations and individuals who observe and document human rights violations, and the pressure involves secret service and EU funding**

Greek police accused ten \(at the time unnamed\) people to have facilitated the ‚Äúillegal entry for foreigners‚Äù\. Those involved are also accused of espionage as well as ‚Äûimpeding investigations‚Äú\. Some of them are also said to have violated immigration law\. However, no arrests or searches have been made so far\. Four of these people had been working for NGOs\.


[![]()](https://www.hrw.org/news/2021/07/22/greek-authorities-target-ngos-reporting-abuses-against-migrants)


The main accusations are literally the content of some of the NGOs‚Äô work:

The suspects are alleged to have operated ‚Äûapplications on the internet‚Äú and informed about ‚Äûmigration flows‚Äú from Turkey\. With the help of ‚Äûtelephone connections, ‚Äú they would have received coordinates of boats with refugees and passed them on to other activists, who would then have observed the course until arrival at the Greek mainland\. During the exchange of information, the number of boat occupants and their medical conditions were also of interest, the officials wisely noted\.

Publishing information, among others, on the illegal pushbacks by the Greek coast guard online via photos and videos, is what some of the organisations are dedicated to doing\. The very same videos are now used as evidence of alleged espionage: The suspects are alleged to have published media that revealed classified information about Greek coast guard vessels and facilities\.

Of course, this is not the first dubious investigation against human rights activists pressured by the Greek authorities\.

In September 2020 the Greek police h [ad made public similar investigations](https://www.kathimerini.gr/society/561103954/i-epicheirisi-alkmini-tis-eyp-gia-tis-mko/) against 35 foreign nationals and members of various organisations from Germany, France, Switzerland, Austria, Norway and Bulgaria with ‚ÄûOperation Alcmene‚Äú\. On the radar of the authorities is, for exmaple, the organisation Alarm Phone/Watch the Med, which is based in several countries and operates an emergency telephone for refugees on the high seas\.

There have of yet been no indictments\.

In addition to the Greek police, the migration ministry, the foreign authorities and the coast guard, the civil\-military domestic secret service EYP, and the anti\-terror department DAEEB are also involved in the investigations\. Through the Directorate for Information Management and Analysis \(DIDAP\), which is also participating and whose establishment [was supported by EU funds in 2016](https://www.ekathimerini.com/news/221327/intelligence-services-to-get-boost/) , information on the persons concerned could be retrieved from EU databases\. The agency [acts as an international contact point](http://www.astynomia.gr/index.php?option=ozo_content&perform=view&id=55028&Itemid=13&lang=EN) for the processing of aircraft passenger data, among other things\.

In the course of the investigation, two mobile phones attributed to two foreign activists, as well as laptops and mobile phones, were seized by the police in September\.

Meanwhile, the secret service should be professional regarding espionage: In the past year it became known that the EYP had sent two spies to Turkey\.

On Wednesday July 21, NGOs including Caritas Europe, Save the Children and Pro Asyl also called on the EU Commission to encourage member states to halt deportations\.

As we have been reporting, on July 11, Afghanistan had asked European countries to cease deportations of Afghan nationals for three months due to an uptick in fighting between state forces and the Taliban\. In response, Finland and [Sweden](https://www.infomigrants.net/en/post/33684/sweden-follows-finland-suspends-deportations-to-afghanistan) recently decided to suspend all deportations to the country\.

Instead of housing people leaving the region given this escalating situation, the EU is weighing a new package of financial aid intended for keeping away people wanting to enter Europe\.


> The EU plan, which is at a preparatory phase, would involve more financial help to Afghanistan, Pakistan and countries in the region, two EU officials and one diplomat familiar with the talks told Reuters, with money also being given to Iran\. 





Turkey, which is set to receive new EU funds to house people leaving the region towards Europe, could play a role in housing more Afghans, but any new [funding for Afghanistan would be separate](https://www.ekathimerini.com/news/1165025/as-war-rages-eu-weighs-more-funds-to-limit-afghans-fleeing-to-bloc/?utm_source=dlvr.it&utm_medium=twitter&fbclid=IwAR1zmiecpjvNkqD69e4f7F3KM81ANWponZBYp8pAYE0QjC1HrXc85l6zt5c) from the planned 3\.5 billion euros for Turkey until 2024\.

#### BELGIUM
### **Over 450 people ended 2\-month hunger strike after government announced willingness to renegotiate**

The strikers demanded asylum negotiations, as most of them come from Morocco and Algeria, which are recognized in the EU as safe countries of origin\. Most strikers lived in Belgium for years remaining unrecognized, their work and life situation worsened by the pandemic\.

Abdeslam, 42, from Algeria:


> ‚ÄúDespite all our skills and the fact that we are here to work, not to just collect money, the answer remains the same, to ‚Äògo home\.‚Äô‚Äù 





The strike began May 23\. After two long months, meetings between Belgian government representatives and those representing the striking people resulted in a breakthrough this Wednesday, when authorities suggested that all the cases would be reassessed without prejudice and that at least some migrants would be able to claim ‚Äúexceptional circumstances‚Äù to remain\.

About 300 people had announced earlier that they were refusing water, with MDM warning that death was possible ‚Äúat any moment‚Äù\. Some had even stitched their lips together earlier this month, challenging government inaction\.

Pressure on the government increased days earlier, when some partners of the governing coalition threatened to withdraw their ministers if any of the hunger strikers died, which put the government at risk of falling\.


> At the moment it is still unclear how many people will ultimately gain the right to remain in the country\. 




Meanwhile, some people had to be wheeled out of the places they had been occupying, such as the Church of St\. John the Baptist at the Beguinage; many required first aid or were taken to the hospital\.

Each person will reportedly quickly be informed about his or her respective options regarding a residence permit in Belgium, according to reports\.

#### GENERAL
### ISD published a report on Networks and Narratives of Anti\-Refugee Disinformation in Europe

The Institute for Strategic and Dialog released a new [report](https://www.isdglobal.org/digital_dispatches/the-networks-and-narratives-of-anti-refugee-disinformation-in-europe/?fbclid=IwAR3qi3asoI9JKN8-q50S-mnSK31P2b8AURNt0dxtJq3jAiqKQMH0J71v1Dc) analysing different networks associated with anti\-refugee disinformation and hatred in Greek, German and English\.

The research highlights that networks of far\-right extremists and elements of the political right sharing common audiences and using similar tactics do not work in isolation, and reveals the dynamics by which anti\-refugee disinformation is spread\.

Some key findings:
- The Greek so\-called ‚Äúrefugee crisis‚Äù attracted and activated an international cohort of extremist actors\.
- Anti\-refugee disinformation has provided a crucial nexus for a range of far\-right extremists, nativists, and conspiracy theorists to overlap with political actors\.
- Elected officials were found to play a role in the amplification of anti\-refugee disinformation and hatred\.
- The Evros border incident and the fires in camps in Moria highlight how key events can act as flashpoints for transnational, multilingual spikes in anti\-refugee mobilisation\.
- NGOs working with people on the move are common targets for harassment and disinformation, both online and offline\.


As far as broader implications of the analysis, the authors state:


> ‚ÄúThe key findings outlined in this article highlight the way in which networks are activated around key crises, such as events on the Evros border or the fires in Moria, to agitate around long\-established hostilities against refugees, minority groups, and the organisations who are deemed to have helped them\. The fluidity of these networks helps keep migration consistently relevant to a multitude of different extremist ecosystems and makes clear that while migration to Europe has reduced dramatically, anti\-migrant discourse has evolved to focus on the migrants already there\.‚Äù
